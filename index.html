Good morning everyone. We are Team HULE from BSCS 2C. Today we are pitching our project for Software Engineering 1: A web-based Inventory Management System built specifically for our client, Jhaerin Tire Supply.
The Challenge

Problem Overview

Current State

  • Manual, paper-based logbooks
  • Basic disconnected spreadsheets
  • Complex inventory: brands, sizes & treads

The Impact

  • Lost entries & miscounted stock
  • Frequent stockouts or over-ordering
  • No instant financial summaries
The business currently uses manual logbooks and basic spreadsheets to manage their complex inventory of tires varying by brand, size, and tread. This outdated process results in lost records, miscounts, stockouts, and makes computing revenue and gross profit difficult without tedious manual calculation.

Course Instructor

Our platform serves multiple stakeholders. The store owner gets full access to reports and analytics, while store staff get restricted access to handle day-to-day stock records. Our development team and our course instructor represent the execution and evaluation sides of this project.
Business Impact

Why This Matters Now

  • Slow Replenishment: Manual tracking causes delayed alerts and poor restocking decisions.
  • Lost Sales Data: No real-time visibility means missed sales opportunities.
  • Financial Blind Spots: No automated analytics prevents the owner from tracking true profit and inventory value.
Why fix this now? Because the current workarounds cost the business actual money. Without real-time visibility or automated alerts, restocking is slow, sales are lost, and financial tracking is completely blind until the end of the month.
Our Answer

Proposed Solution

A system that digitizes inventory tracking to improve accuracy, efficiency, and real-time monitoring for Jhaerin Tire Supply.
Digitize Tracking
Automate Alerts
Instant Reports
Our proposed solution is a Web-based Inventory Management System. It replaces manual workflows into a structured dashboard that digitizes tracking, automates low stock alerts, and generates business-critical reports instantly.

Scope Management

Scope & Prioritization

We use the MoSCoW framework to manage scope and ensure core value is delivered first.

Must Have

Non-negotiable core functionality required for the system to operate.

Should Have

Important, high-value features that enhance operational efficiency.

Could Have

Nice-to-have features for future sprints if time permits.

Won't Have

Out of scope for this term's delivery.

To manage our development time actively, we utilized the MoSCoW prioritization framework. This guarantees that basic functional needs are met before adding complex enhancements.
Priority: Critical

Must Have Features

  • User login & authentication FR-01
  • Create product records (brand, size, tread) FR-03, 04
  • Record stock-in and stock-out logs FR-05, 06
  • Real-time inventory quantity display FR-07
  • Analytics dashboard overview FR-08
  • Generate daily inventory reports FR-09
Our Must-Haves include the foundation: secure login, creating products like tires with specific treads and sizes, logging stock-in/out, viewing real time quantities, and generating simple daily reports.
Priority: Important

Should Have Features

  • Staff account management FR-02
  • Weekly/Monthly sales reports FR-10, 11
  • Advanced filtering (brand, size) FR-12
  • Top-selling / Slow-moving analytics FR-13, 14
  • Revenue & gross profit computation FR-15, 16
  • Low-stock & dead-stock alerts (60 days) FR-21, 22
Our Should-Haves focus on business intelligence: staff management, deeper filtering, identifying top-selling versus dead stock tires, automating profit computation, and critical low-stock threshold alerts.
